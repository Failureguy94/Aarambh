How LifeLink Works

1

Create Your Profile

Sign up as a donor, patient, or healthcare provider. Complete your profile with relevant medical information and verification details.

2

Verification Process

Hospitals and blood banks go through a verification process to ensure all requests are legitimate and all donors are eligible.

3

Blood Request

When blood is needed, hospitals can create a request specifying blood type, quantity, and urgency level.

4

Donor Matching

Our system automatically finds eligible donors in the vicinity and sends notifications based on proximity and donor availability.

5

Donation Coordination

Once a donor accepts, the system facilitates communication between the donor and the hospital for a smooth donation process.

Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ Item
Who can donate blood?
Most healthy individuals aged 18–65, weighing at least 50kg, can donate blood. However, eligibility depends on factors like health, travel history, and medications.
How often can I donate blood?
You can donate whole blood every 56 days (approximately every 2 months). For platelets or plasma, the interval may differ.
Is blood donation safe?
Yes, donating blood is safe. All equipment is sterile and used only once. Trained professionals ensure donor safety.
Will I feel weak after donating?
Some people may feel lightheaded briefly. It’s important to rest, stay hydrated, and eat something after donating.
How does your platform match donors and patients?
Our system uses location-based matching and real-time alerts to connect donors with nearby patients or hospitals in need.
